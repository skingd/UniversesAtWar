## No Guts, No Galaxy
This is recognized by long time BattleTech players asthe core philosophy of BattleTech. It emphasizes the importance of risk and reward in the game, where players must carefully manage their resources and make strategic decisions to achieve victory. Units in BattleTech have copious amounts of firepower at their disposal, but must contend with many negative modifiers, such as range, movement, heat, visibility, weather effects and more. By pushing the limits, taking chances on cluch shosts or waiting for the perfect moment to strike, players are managing risk vs reward. While Legions Imperialis cannot capture the full depth of BattleTech's mechanics, it operates at an entirely new level of tactical abstraction. Instead of managing a lance or company of units, avoiding ice fields and tracking heat levels, players are now operating closer to the battalion level. Entire companies of units, supported by artillery, air and infantry are now available in a single, lightly constrained battlefield. I've endeavored to capture the spirit of "No Guts, No Galaxy" while expanding the scope of the rules.

## Heat Mechanics

In BattleTech, heat is a critical game mechanic that regulates the pace of the game and tactical decisions. These mechanics are both detailed and created to emphasize the "No Guts, No galaxy" motto of BattleTech. By pushing the limits of heat management, players can achieve powerful effects but at the risk of overheating and suffering penalties, and in some case disasterous consequences.

Within the scope of Legions Imperials, heat mechanics are simplified and streamlined to fit the faster pace of the game, while still maintaining strategic depth. Specifically, they balance the firepower contained in BattleTech units against the factions of the Warhammer 30k setting.

### Heat Generation and Management
Each mech in has a heat threshold that represents the maximum amount of heat it can generate safely each turn. Heat is generated by firing most weapons, taking certain actions, or being hit by certain types of weapons. Heat does not carry over between turns, but if a mech generates more heat than its threahold in a single turn, it suffers an automatic wound with no save of any type allowed.

### Heat from Enemy Fire
Certain weapons will add heat counters if they hit a mech. The following will increase heat by +1 if they hit a mech:
- Flamers
- Heavy Flamers
- Any weapon with Firestorm or Inferno traits
- Phosphex
- Plasma weapons

### Heat Effects
Every time a mech generates heat, add that to its total heat generation for the turn. For example, if a mech runs, that would be +1 heat and if it fires a PPC, that would be +3 heat.

**Overheating**: If a mech generates an amount of heat greater than its heat threshold in a turn, it is considered to be overheating. At the end of a player's turn, for each mech that has one or more heat heat points above its heat threshold, roll a d6. If the result is higher than the number of heat points that exceed the threshold, then there is no further effect. If the result is equal to or lower than the number of heat points that exceed the threshold, then the mech suffers a wound with no saves allowed.

For example, if the mech's heat threshold is 5, and it generates 6 heat in a turn, the owning player would roll a d6. If the result is 2 or higher, there is no futher effect. If the result is 1, the mech suffers a wound.

If heat exceeds the threshold by 6 or more, the mech automatically takes a wound.

If this would remove the last remaining wound from the mech, it is considered destroyed and removed from play.

**Effects of heat on vehicles, Aerospace units and infantry**: Heat does not affect vehicles, aerospace units or infantry in any way. They are not subject to heat generation or overheating.

## Weapon Rules
**A-Pod**: Directed shrapnel weapons fitted to mech's legs, these are used to cut down infantry trying attack a mech in melee. Once per game, if an enemy unit is in base contact with a mech equipped with an A-Pod, and before any other attackes are made, that mech can immediately make a single attack against the enemy unit with the A-Pod. Resolve casualties before rolling for combat.

Rule: Once per game, if an enemy unit is in base contact with a mech equipped with an A-Pod, and before any other attackes are made, that mech can immediately make a single attack against the enemy unit with the A-Pod. Resolve casualties before rolling for combat.

**B-Pod**: Similar to the A-Pod, but redesigned to take out battle armor using cluster munitions. They function exactly like A-Pods.

Rule: Once per game, if an enemy unit is in base contact with a mech equipped with a B-Pod, and before any other attackes are made, that mech can immediately make a single attack against the enemy unit with the B-Pod. Resolve casualties before rolling for combat.

**TAG**: Target Acquisition Gear allows units to designate targets for other friendly units to lock onto. Roll to hit as normal against the target, but no damage is done or saves are made. Instead, the target is considered to be "painted" for the rest of the turn. Any friendly unit that targets a painted unit does not recieve an indirect fire penalty. It is also considered to be "painted" for the purposes of any weapon or ability that interacts with painted targets, such as copperhead rounds.

Rule: Roll to hit as normal against the target, but no damage is done or saves are made. Instead, the target is considered to be "painted" for the rest of the turn. Any friendly unit that targets a painted unit does not recieve an indirect fire penalty. It is also considered to be "painted" for the purposes of any weapon or ability that interacts with painted targets, such as copperhead rounds.

**Weapon Type**: There are three weapon types that are used for ranged weapons on mechs: energy, ballistic and missile. These types are used to determine the interactions of weapons with certain equipment, such as targeting computers and ECM. A weapon is considered to be the weapon type listed in its profile.

Rule: A weapon is considered to be the weapon type listed in its profile.

**Narc**: Narc systems are a type of missile beacon that attatches to a target on contact and begins transmitting. This allows much easier locking for missile weapons and makes it more difficult for the target to hide or evade attacks.

The Narc system does no damage on a successful hit. Instead, place a narc beacon on a single model in an enemy detachment if a hit is scored. All missile weapons that target that detachment can be allocated to the model with the beacon as if they were precision shots. In addition, they do not suffer from any indirect fire penalties and gain +1 to hit. ECM Suites block the effect of Narc beacons.

**INarc**: Improved Narc systems are an advanced version of the standard Narc system that provides even greater benefits to missile weapon accuracy. They function exactly like normal Narc systems, but in addition to the standard Narc effects, all missile weapons get +1 to-hit against the target and ECM Suites have no effect (though Angel ECM Suites still block INarc).


## Equipment Rules

**Artemis IV FCS**: The Artemis IV system is a targeting computer that provides a significant boost to the accuracy of missile weapons. It allows the user to ignore one level of range penalty when firing missile weapons, and also provides a +1 bonus to hit rolls with missile weapons. This makes it an essential piece of equipment for any mech that relies on missile weaponry, as it can greatly increase the effectiveness of those weapons.

Rule: +1 to hit rolls with missile weapons.

**Artemis V FCS**: The Artemis V system is an advanced targeting computer that provides an even greater boost to the accuracy of missile weapons than the Artemis IV. +1 to hit with missile weapons and +1 dice. It This makes it an invaluable piece of equipment for any mech that relies heavily on missile weaponry, as it can significantly enhance the performance of those weapons.

Rule: +1 to hit rolls with missile weapons and +1 dice.

**ECM Suite**: ECM (Electronic Countermeasures) Suites distrupt targetting systems by amplifying the mech's electronic signature, making it harder for enemy units to lock onto it. It works on the same pricnipal as shinging a spotlight in the enemy's eyes to blind them. This provides a significant defensive advantage, as it can reduce the accuracy of enemy attacks against the unit, and any friendly units near it. Any weapon that targets a unit with ECM, or a detachment that includes a unit with ECM, loses the accurate and Tracking traits.

In addition, NARC, Artemis IV and Artemis V systems have no bonus agains units under the protection of ECM.

Rule: Any weapon that targets a unit with ECM, or a unit with ECM, loses the accurate and Tracking traits. Narc, Artimes IV and Artemis V lose all bonuses against units under the protection of ECM.

**Angel ECM Suite**: The Angel ECM Suite is an advanced version of the standard ECM Suite that provides even greater protection against enemy targeting systems. In addition to the standard ECM effects, Angel ECM blocks Bloodhound Active Probe and INarc.

**AMS**: Anti-Missile Systems (AMS) attempt to shoot down incoming missiles before they can hit their target. Whenever a unit with AMS is targeted by a missile weapon, that unit gets an immediate 5+ save against the missile weapon's attacks. This is before the unit's normal armor saves are rolled. This is reduced to a 6+ against any missile fired from a titan.

Rule: Whenever a unit with AMS is targeted by a missile weapon, that unit gets an immediate 5+ saving throw against the missile weapon's attacks. This is before the unit's normal armor saves are rolled. The save is reduced to a 6+ against any missile fired from a titan.

**Laser AMS** : Laser Anti-Missile Systems (Laser AMS) are an advanced form of AMS that use high-powered lasers to shoot down incoming missiles. Whenever a unit with Laser AMS is targeted by a missile weapon, that unit gets an immediate 4+ save against the missile weapon's attacks. This is before the unit's normal armor saves are rolled. This is reduced to a 5+ against any missile fired from a titan.

Each use of Laser AMS increases heat by +1.

Rule: Whenever a unit with Laser AMS is targeted by a missile weapon, that unit gets an immediate 4+ saving throw against the missile weapon's attacks. This is before the unit's normal armor saves are rolled. The save is reduced to a 5+ against any missile fired from a titan.

**MASC**: MASC (Myomer Accelerator Signal Circuit) is a piece of equipment that allows a mech to temporarily boost its speed and mobility. When activated, MASC provides a significant increase to the mech's movement speed for a short duration, allowing it to quickly close the distance with enemies or retreat from danger. However, using MASC generates a large amount of heat, so it must be used strategically to avoid overheating. MASC can be activated whenever an advance or march order is issued to the mech, and its effects last until the end of the turn. While MASC is active, the mech's movement speed is increased according to the chart below. For every inch moved beyond the mech's normal movement range, it generates +1 heat.

Light Mechs: +6"
Medium Mechs: +4"
Heavy Mechs: +3"
Assault Mechs: +2"

Rule: MASC can be activated whenever an advance or march order is issued to the mech, and its effects last until the end of the turn. While MASC is active, the mech's movement speed is increased according to the chart above. For every inch moved beyond the mech's normal movement range, it generates +1 heat.

**Targeting Computer**: Targeting Computers provide a significant boost to the accuracy of a mech's weapons. When firing with the unit's weapons, the owning player may select one of the following rules to use for all of the firing weapons:
+ Recieve +1 bonus to-hit with all Energy and Ballistic weapons that are firing.
+ Receive a -1 penalty to hit with all energy and ballistic weapons but gain the precision strike trait with those weapons.

Rule: When firing with the unit's weapons, the owning player may select a targeting rule to use for all of the effected firing weapons.

**Beagle Active Probe (BAP)**: Advanced sensor systems that is able to detect the smallest trace of energy or movement, providing a significant boost to the unit's detection abilities. Enemy units may not infiltrate within 12" of a unit with BAP.

Rule: Enemy units may not infiltrate within 12" of a unit with BAP.

**Apollo FCS**: The Apollo Fire Control System is a dedicated targeting computer for the MRM weapon system. Any MRM weapon fired by a unit with an Apollo FCS loses 1 dice but gains +1 to hit.

Rule: Any MRM weapon fired by a unit with an Apollo FCS loses 1 dice but gains +1 to hit.

**Actuator Enhancement System (AES)**: The AES is an improved set of actuators that allow for more precise movements. Mechs with AES gain +1 to their CAF and +1 to ranged to-hit rolls on ranged attacks. Light and Medium mechs also gain nimble.

Rule: Mechs with AES gain +1 to their CAF and +1 to ranged to-hit rolls on ranged attacks. Light and Medium mechs also gain nimble.

**CASE**: CASE (Cellular Ammunition Storage Equipment) is a safety system that prevents ammunition from exploding when a unit is hit. If a unit with CASE is hit by a weapon that does additional wounds, reduce those additional wounds by 1.

Rule: If a unit with CASE is hit by a weapon that does additional wounds, reduce those additional wounds by 1.

**CASE II**: An improved version of CASE that provides even greater protection against ammunition explosions. If a unit with CASE II is hit by a weapon that does additional wounds, reduce those additional wounds by d3.

Rule: If a unit with CASE II is hit by a weapon that does additional wounds, reduce those additional wounds by d3.

**Jump Jets**: Jump Jets are fusion powered thrusters that allow mechs to quickly move to higher positions or dodge incoming attacks. The elevation gain is relatively low and cannot be sustained, but is nevertheless effective at quickly relocating. The mech gains the skimmer special rule, can move half its movement and gains +1 heat. It can gain +2 heat to gain its full movement as a skimmer.

Rule: The mech gains the skimmer special rule, can move half its movement and gains +1 heat. It can gain +2 heat to gain its full movement as a skimmer.

**Improved Jump Jets**: They function exactly like normal Jump Jets, but generate only +1 heat regardless of how far the mech moves.

Rule: The mech gains the skimmer special rule and gains +1 heat.

**Coolant Pod**: A large resevoir of coolant fluid that can be used to flush through the mech's systems to rapidly reduce heat. Once per game, before rolling for heat effects, a mech with a coolant pod can reduce its total heat by 1d6.

Rule: Once per game, before rolling for heat effects, a mech with a coolant pod can reduce its total heat by 1d6.
